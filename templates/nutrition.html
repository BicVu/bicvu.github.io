Behavior and Public Policies on Nutrition

We shift gears in looking at the connection between nutrition and obesity since there has been a public focus on the health of youth across America. We utilized data from the CDCâ€™s Youth Risk Behavior Surveillance System (YRBSS) and other public data to look the availability of sugary food items and its potential correlation with consumption. We mapped public policies across different states that allowed students to purchase soda pop or fruit drinks from one or more vending machines or at the school store, canteen, or snack bar. However, the data was incomplete and are not regularly mandated across all states, therefore it is difficult to draw any patterns between the availability of sugar food items and obesity.

The charts of sugar intake and fruits and vegetable consumption does illustrate a correlation between diet and obesity levels. There is a regular pattern that shows youths who consume a higher amount of soda also do not eat as many fruits and vegetables, and are also among populations with high numbers of obesity.
